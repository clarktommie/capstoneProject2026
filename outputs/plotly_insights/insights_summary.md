# Plotly Insight Summary

## National Risk Regime
- Latest year 2025: rate is 0.30 per 100k (-0.39 vs prior year).
- Peak incident rate in the available series is 0.71 per 100k in 2023.
- Latest enrollment YoY change is -0.35%, useful for separating incident-count changes from population changes.

## State Burden vs Volatility
- States in the upper-right quadrant combine high recent burden and high volatility; these are strongest candidates for near-term risk monitoring.
- Top states by recent burden/volatility profile: CA (19.2 avg, vol 5.4), TX (18.4 avg, vol 5.4), OH (16.4 avg, vol 5.5), NY (15.0 avg, vol 4.6), IL (14.6 avg, vol 4.6).
- Fastest-rising recent trajectories: FL (slope 2.20/yr), NE (slope 1.50/yr), WA (slope 1.40/yr).

## Policy Overlap View
- Using the most recent five years (2019-2023), 4 states land in both the top-burden and top-policy-coverage quartiles.
- High-burden states outside the dense-policy group identify potential policy or implementation gaps: FL, GA, NC, NY, OH, PA.
- States with dense policy coverage but lower recent burden may be useful for comparative case studies: CO, CT, DC, DE, MA, NJ.
- Policy coverage is based on 10 binary law series merged to state-year data.
